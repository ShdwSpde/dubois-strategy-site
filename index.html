Why Du Bois First

Du Bois was the first sociologist to use data visualization as a tool for racial justice — his 1900 Paris Exposition charts are now canonical in data science. He published 27 major works. His papers span UMass Amherst, Fisk, and the Schomburg Center. He spent his final years in Accra, Ghana, where the Du Bois Centre stands today. He is the perfect proof of concept: a vast body of work, a physical heritage site, and a legacy that demands immersive, interactive treatment.

What We Proved

  • RAG architecture works across a full bibliography (17 works indexed)
  • WebXR delivers immersive VR experiences in any browser — no app downloads
  • Interactive curriculum modules map cleanly to K-12 and university standards
  • Same architecture is directly replicable for the next figure
  • Full market sizing, GTM, and 5-year financial model completed

The Equity Argument

The immersive technology industry is building experiences about everything except the African diaspora. We don't add representation to someone else's platform — we build the platform itself, owned and operated by the communities whose stories it tells. Every VR experience is created by African creators. Every archive makes scholarship more accessible, not more expensive. Every headset placed in an HBCU classroom is a free license. This is not diversity theater. This is infrastructure.
