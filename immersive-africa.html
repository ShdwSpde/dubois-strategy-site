Every projection is built from unit economics. Here is the bottom-up methodology for each revenue stream, showing Year 1 baselines scaling to Year 5.

12-week intensive VR courses delivered via Wi-Flix studios. Fee-paying cohorts of up to 20 participants.

Y1: 2 cohorts × 10 students × $1,600/student = $320K
Y5: 6 cohorts × 20 students × $3,500/student = $4.2M
Scaling: add advanced courses, corporate training, international virtual cohorts

Ticketing & Tours

In-person and virtual tours of the Du Bois Center. Benchmarked against Nkrumah Memorial Park (333K visitors/yr).

Y5: 80K visitors × $25 avg (VR premium) + virtual = $4M
Benchmark: KNMP 333K visitors (GTA 2024) ~$7 local / ~$15 foreign

Expansion Potential

Beyond Du Bois: The Nkrumah Opportunity

The Du Bois platform is the proof of concept. Kwame Nkrumah is the expansion that proves the model is replicable — and that Immersive Africa can become the definitive platform for Pan-African cultural heritage.

The Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park in Accra is Ghana's most visited tourist attraction — 333,233 visitors in 2024 alone, following a $3.5M renovation completed in July 2023. Yet no immersive VR experience of the site exists. No living archive spans his 16 published works. No interactive curriculum brings his Pan-African vision to digital life.

Nkrumah authored foundational texts on decolonization, Pan-Africanism, and African unity — from Ghana: The Autobiography (1957) to Neo-Colonialism: The Last Stage of Imperialism (1965) and Consciencism (1964). His papers are held across six major archives on three continents, including 26 linear feet at Howard University's Moorland-Spingarn Research Center.

Most critically: the Du Bois Centre and the Nkrumah Memorial are 3-5 km apart in Accra. Du Bois died in Ghana in 1963 at the personal invitation of Nkrumah. They co-organized the Fifth Pan-African Congress in Manchester in 1945. Their legacies are inseparable.

The Du Bois–Nkrumah Connection

Their legacies are inseparable. No immersive experience tells this connected story.

1945Du Bois chairs the Fifth Pan-African Congress in Manchester; Nkrumah co-organizes. Du Bois is 77, Nkrumah is 36.
1957Ghana becomes the first sub-Saharan African nation to gain independence. Nkrumah invites Du Bois — but the US has confiscated his passport.
1961Nkrumah formally invites Du Bois to Ghana to direct the Encyclopedia Africana. Du Bois, age 93, relocates permanently.
1963Du Bois becomes a Ghanaian citizen. He dies in Accra on August 27 — the eve of the March on Washington. Nkrumah co-founds the OAU.
TodayBoth memorialized and buried in Accra, 3-5 km apart. Neither site has an immersive digital experience.

Why This Matters for Scale

Du Bois proves the model works. Nkrumah proves it's replicable. Every component uses the same technical architecture, same content pipeline, same distribution via Wi-Flix. After Nkrumah: Fela Kuti, Wangari Maathai, Thomas Sankara, Miriam Makeba. Immersive Africa becomes a platform, not a project.
