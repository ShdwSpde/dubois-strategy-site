Customer Personas

Four Buyers. One Sequential Engine.

Creators generate awareness. HBCUs build credibility. Institutions write checks. Pan-African partners deliver scale. The order matters.

◆

Dr. Aisha Coleman

Museum Director / University Gallery Curator
Age 42-55 $85-145K income PhD/MFA DC / NYC / ATL

Pain Points

  • 55% of museums still below pre-pandemic attendance; board pressure to innovate
  • Last VR pilot took 6 months of IT negotiation and still felt gimmicky
  • Black history programming relegated to February — needs year-round substance
  • Exhibition budgets 15-25% below 2019; every dollar justifies itself to the board
  • Content pipeline thin — everything is another Van Gogh retread

Why She Buys

  • Wants an exhibition that breaks attendance records and generates organic press
  • Needs turnkey deployment: preloaded headsets, no IT burden
  • Decision: committee-based, 3-9 months, partially grant-funded
  • WTP: $150K-$500K for full exhibition + hardware + licensing bundle
  • Becomes an evangelist if the experience delivers on attendance + press + educator feedback

Top Objections

  • "Is this a real exhibition or a tech demo?" — Needs scholarly rigor evidence
  • "Can our infrastructure handle this?" — Previous vendor left broken headsets
  • "Will it last beyond the novelty?" — Needs 3-6 month engagement depth

Segment Data

  • 45% of total SAM (~5,500 museums + 195 universities)
  • 67% of projected 5-year revenue ($52M)
  • Priority: #1 — revenue engine (activated Phase 2 after HBCU credibility)
  • Discovery: AAM conference, peer recommendations, Hyperallergic features

Prof. Marcus Jefferson

HBCU Department Chair / Dean of Humanities
Age 48-62 $95-160K income PhD ATL / Nashville / DC

Pain Points

  • Entire department tech budget less than a single PWI lab (129:1 endowment gap)
  • Students are digital natives learning on a 2014 LMS
  • Can't hire a digital humanist; needs tools that work without new hires
  • HBCU research gets fraction of PWI media coverage
  • Burned by ed-tech vendors who collect case studies and disappear

Why He Partners

  • Free perpetual license is expected, not a perk — endowment reality demands it
  • Wants students presenting VR research at national conferences
  • Grant-funded purchases bypass procurement barriers (2-4 months vs 6-12)
  • If students produce work and get jobs because of the tool, he tells every HBCU president
  • WTP: $0 for license; $5-15K for training; $25K for VR Lab if grant-funded

Top Objections

  • "We can't afford another subscription that expires" — Free perpetual is essential
  • "Are you going to use our students as a case study and leave?" — Needs sustained commitment
  • "Does this work without reliable campus Wi-Fi?" — Offline mode non-negotiable

Segment Data

  • 20% of SAM (107 HBCUs + connected orgs)
  • 10% of revenue ($8M, mostly grant-funded)
  • Priority: #1 tied — credibility engine that unlocks all other segments
  • Discovery: HBCU word-of-mouth, ASALH conference, colleague email

Jordan Nakamura-Osei

Creative Director / Immersive Experience Designer
Age 28-38 $75-200K income BFA/MFA NYC / LA / London / Remote

Pain Points

  • Immersive space saturated with spectacle, starved of substance
  • Projects that matter most pay the least; constantly patching funding
  • Sees immersive art built about communities without their governance
  • WebXR portability vs. performance tradeoffs are real
  • No "senior immersive artist" career ladder; freelances by default

Why They Join

  • Not a buyer — a creator, partner, and distribution channel
  • One believer produces $50-200K in content and more marketing than $500K in ads
  • Wants fellowship ($10-20K), Ghana residency, or commission ($10-50K per experience)
  • Creator revenue share: 15-30% of content licensing revenue their work generates
  • The Ghana lab is designed to create 60 Jordans per year

Top Objections

  • "Am I executing someone else's vision?" — Needs creative ownership
  • "What's the economic model for creators?" — Revenue share must be real, not "exposure"
  • "Will anyone actually see this?" — Tour + hardware distribution is compelling if credible

Segment Data

  • 10% of SAM (~5,000 active XR cultural creators)
  • 7% of revenue ($5M content + advocacy value)
  • Priority: #1 tied — fastest to activate, lowest cost, outsized awareness impact
  • Discovery: Tribeca, SXSW, XR Discord, Voices of VR, peer social posts

  • His endorsement opens doors no cold email can; his rejection closes them permanently

Top Objections

  • "Can this scale in African infrastructure?" — Offline headsets are essential
  • "What happens to creators after training?" — Content-to-headset pipeline must be real

Segment Data

  • 25% of SAM (~500 African cultural institutions + diaspora networks)
  • 16% of revenue ($12M — Ghana lab + FESTAC + hardware)
  • Priority: #2 — long game, cultivate Day 1, convert Phase 3
